FieldSense

Soil · weather · signal

FieldSense turns soil, weather, and an optional leaf photo into clear suggestions you can take to your agronomist. Nothing vague—just structured answers you can act on.

You tell it about your field

  • What’s your soil like? (pH, moisture, nutrients such as nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium — N, P, K)
  • What’s the weather like? (temperature, rainfall, humidity)
  • Optionally, upload one clear photo (leaf or canopy) for disease prediction.

Then FieldSense tells you

  • Which crop fits your soil and climate numbers
  • Which fertilizer direction lines up with your soil, crop, and lab-style nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium (N, P, K)
  • What a single clear leaf or canopy photo most likely points to
  • A quick health read and plain-language why behind each suggestion

Expert in your pocket

Think of it as soil-science vocabulary with a memory: every run is saved so you can see how recommendations shift as your numbers change.

Compared with only asking a human

  • Consistent — the same numbers yield the same structured answer every time.
  • Transparent — you see what went in and how the app reasoned, not a black-box paragraph.
  • Grounded — suggestions come from trained models on crop, soil, and (when you use a photo) leaf data—not free-form guessing.
  • Tracked over time — each field keeps a timeline so you can compare this month to last.

Farmer: “My field has pH 7.2, moisture about 60%, and potassium reads low.”

FieldSense: “Given those numbers, wheat is a strong crop match; lean toward a potassium-forward fertilizer plan; overall field health from the rollup might land in the high 60s to 70s—good, but keep an eye on irrigation and re-check potassium (K) after the next soil test.”

Next month you update the numbers → new predictions stack next to the old ones so you can see whether things improved.

Summary

Crop fit, fertilizer direction, photo-based plant checks, and next steps—together in one lightweight app you can run yourself or host.
